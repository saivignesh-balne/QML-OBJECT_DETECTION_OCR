INTERNSHIP EVALUATION PRESENTATION SCRIPT
Project: Hybrid Quantum Machine Learning for Object Detection Research and OCR
Date: 10 April 2026

Slide 1. Title
Script:
Good morning. This internship project focuses on Quantum Machine Learning, or QML, in a practical object-detection setting. To study that properly, I built a complete pipeline for object detection, classification, and OCR, and then used it as a benchmark platform to compare strong classical models with hybrid quantum models under the same workflow.

Slide 2. Agenda and Presentation Context
Script:
This presentation covers the project background, the research objective, the system architecture, the methods and technologies used, the internship objectives achieved, the benchmark findings, and the final recommendation. The central idea is not to assume that quantum is better, but to measure where QML is useful and where classical methods still remain stronger.

Slide 3. Project Context and Problem Definition
Script:
The project is best described as QML object-detection research and development. The current benchmark uses three object categories, namely bottles, chip packets, and medicine boxes, but these are the experimental classes rather than the project identity. The real objective is to evaluate QML inside a realistic object-detection and classification pipeline.

Slide 4. End-to-End System Architecture
Script:
The pipeline begins with preprocessing, followed by object detection, ROI extraction, classification, and OCR. The most important research decision is that the comparison happens at the classifier stage. That means the same computer-vision front end is preserved while classical and hybrid quantum models are evaluated fairly on the same ROI features.

Slide 5. Experimental Workflow and User Interface
Script:
The project includes a complete UI for training, benchmarking, and inference. This makes the system reproducible and presentation-ready. It also supports saved-model reuse, multi-model comparison, and end-to-end demonstrations. So the project is not only a technical prototype, but a structured evaluation environment for QML R&D.

Slide 6. Dataset Design and Evaluation Setup
Script:
Separate datasets were used for detection and ROI classification. The detector was trained on full-scene images with YOLO annotations, while the classifier was trained on cropped ROI images organized by class. This separation is important because detection and classification solve different problems, and it helps keep the comparison technically correct.

Slide 7. Pipeline Methods and Implementation Blocks
Script:
The pipeline uses OpenCV for preprocessing, YOLO for detection, ROI extraction for object isolation, and Tesseract plus TrOCR for OCR. On the classifier side, the project compares both classical and quantum-oriented models using the same evaluation logic. This creates a clear and fair benchmark setup.

Slide 8. Hybrid QML Design and Feature Encoding
Script:
The project uses a hybrid quantum design rather than a pure quantum pipeline. That is because direct quantum handling of raw object images is not practical for this research setting today. Instead, preprocessing and feature extraction remain classical, while the classifier stage becomes the point where QML is introduced. Both angle encoding and amplitude encoding were implemented so classical features could be transformed into quantum-compatible representations.

Slide 9. Comparison Framework
Script:
The classical approach keeps the full decision path in standard machine learning. The hybrid quantum approach keeps the pipeline classical up to feature extraction, then introduces quantum models at the classifier stage. Pure quantum image handling is treated as a future direction because it is not practical for this image task in the current setting.

Slide 10. Technologies Used and Skills Developed
Script:
The project combines object detection, computer vision, OCR, machine learning, quantum machine learning, UI development, and reporting. The main technologies used were Python, OpenCV, PyTorch, Ultralytics YOLO, Qiskit, Qiskit Machine Learning, Tesseract, TrOCR, Flask, and React. The most important skill developed during the internship was learning how to evaluate QML inside a real engineering workflow rather than in isolation.

Slide 11. Achievement of Internship Objectives I
Script:
In terms of application, I built a complete working pipeline. In terms of knowledge, I learned how quantum feature encoding and QML classifiers fit into a practical hybrid design. In terms of exploration, I tested multiple model families, encodings, and kernels. In terms of identification, I determined where QML is competitive and where classical methods remain stronger.

Slide 12. Achievement of Internship Objectives II
Script:
In terms of innovation, I created a hybrid classical-quantum benchmark platform rather than a single-model solution. In terms of engagement, I worked across detection, OCR, QML, UI, benchmarking, and documentation. In terms of evaluation, I used the same benchmark logic across all models. In terms of demonstration, I prepared a presentation-ready workflow with saved artifacts, charts, tables, and a usable interface.

Slide 13. Detector Performance and All-Model Accuracy Comparison
Script:
The detector achieved strong performance, including precision of 0.90054, recall of 0.94974, mAP at 50 of 0.95593, and mAP at 50 to 95 of 0.61159. The classifier chart on this slide shows every available model currently benchmarked in the project. The strongest classical models achieved 99.5 percent accuracy, and the amplitude-encoding QSVM also achieved 99.5 percent. This is one of the most important findings of the internship.

Slide 14. Formal Benchmark Table and Recommendation
Script:
This slide provides the complete model table rather than only the top performers. It shows the full comparison across accuracy, macro F1, and training time. The benchmark leads to a balanced conclusion. For deployment, the strongest current recommendation is the classical stack, specifically YOLO with Classical SVM and OCR ensemble. For research, the strongest quantum result is the amplitude-encoding QSVM, which is highly competitive in accuracy but much slower to train.

Slide 15. Conclusion and Future Scope
Script:
The final conclusion is that this internship successfully delivered both a working AI system and a rigorous QML comparison platform. Classical models currently remain the best deployment choice, while hybrid QML remains the most important research and innovation direction. Future work includes stronger end-to-end validation, improved OCR robustness, and deeper evaluation of additional quantum kernels, encodings, and feature-compression strategies.
